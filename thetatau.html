Who We Are

"The purpose of Theta Tau is to develop and maintain a high standard of professional interest among its member and to unite them in a strong bond of fraternal fellowship."
 
Theta Tau is currently the University of Pennsylvania's only coed professional engineering fraternity. The purpose of Theta Tau is to develop and maintain a high standard of professional interest among its members, and to unite them in a strong bond of fraternal fellowship. Our three pillars of professional development, brotherhood, and service are reflected in our committee structure to keep membership fresh and enthusiastic. Please read on to learn more about the professional, social, and service oriented aspects of our fraternity. As a professional organization, our Chapter aims to develop the technical, useful, and marketable skills of its members through events such as skill-building workshops, résumé reviews, research panels, career fairs, and networking with professors, corporate representatives, and recent alumni. Like a social fraternity, our Chapter organizes many social events for both members as well as for the engineering school as a whole. We have hosted dinners, competitions, parties, intramural sports, and other events. Like an honor society and service organization, our Chapter does more than just uphold a high standard of academic integrity and achievement. We are heavily involved in the city of Philadelphia and in the world beyond; in addition to executing our own projects, Theta Tau includes members and leaders of local mentoring and tutoring projects, leadership workshops, and peer-counseling teams as well as established civic service initiatives including Engineers Without Borders, Model United Nations, MEChA, and others.
